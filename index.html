Werewolf romance audio drama. Merida Cooper — wolfless, cursed, disowned — is claimed at the Lunar Ball by Alpha Darian Oaksmith, future Werewolf King. He married her for reasons he won't explain. She refuses to break. Over 400 episodes a forced marriage becomes devotion, a powerless girl becomes Queen, and a world full of enemies tries to stop it from happening.

The show doesn't stay in one gear. It opens in humiliation and victimhood, pivots at ep 50 into training and rivalry, deepens into slow burn intimacy from ep 100, explodes into political warfare at ep 200, and ends with war, coronation, and a fully transformed protagonist.

The core promise: she doesn't just survive — she becomes someone who goes to war for him.
Key Characters
Protagonist
Merida Cooper
Wolfless, scarred, disowned. Silver hair, violet eyes. Has Fae blood + dormant wolf (Valmora) hidden by the curse. Transforms from passive victim → warrior → Queen.
Male Lead
Darian Oaksmith
Future Werewolf King. Cold, feared, calculating. His wolf Rhovan knows Merida is their mate from ep 1. Darian spends ~100 eps denying it while protecting her anyway.
Primary Rival — Eps 9–374
Wendy Felton
Groomed since childhood to be Darian's Queen. Capable, dangerous, obsessive. The show's most sustained antagonist — drives conflict for 250+ consecutive episodes.
Darian's Brother / Trainer
Danny Oaksmith
Comic relief + Merida's combat trainer from ep 50. Becomes Alpha of Mystic Fury. Her found-family anchor. Mates with Hyacinth.
Political Villain
Reggie Felton
Council Elder. Architect of Wendy's obsession. Schemes against Merida for 200+ eps. Stripped of noble title and placed under house arrest on day 1 of Darian's reign.
Darian's Inner Wolf
Rhovan
"Mine. Protect her." Near-separate character. Recognises Merida from ep 1 and overrules Darian's cold logic for 100+ episodes. Key voice in the possessive alpha trope.
Story Arc Structure
Eps 1–50
Rejection + Forced Marriage
Lunar Ball, Marc's rejection, Darian's claim, chicken coop, forced wedding. Merida refuses I do. Hostile pack, no allies.
Eps 51–100
Duskmoor + Training
Combat training with Danny. First signs of Valmora. Wendy's active scheming. Kidnap attempt. Darian defends Merida publicly.
Eps 101–150
War + Wolf Awakens
Vampire attacks. Human lab killings. Valmora heard by Darian. Mate bond undeniable. Council confrontation. First physical closeness.
Eps 151–200
Marking + Alliance
Merida marks Darian. Vampire alliance. Full-scale war. Political vindication at Council. Slow burn peaks.
Eps 201–250
Stormveil + Fae Blood
Royal court politics. Randolf obstacle. Grandmother letters reveal Fae bloodline. Darian consults Merida. Peak intimacy.
Eps 251–300
Power + Pregnancy
Reggie's most dangerous moves. Grandmother arrives. Twins pregnancy. Wendy turns violent. Coronation prep.
Eps 301–350
Coronation + Purge
Crowned King + Queen. Council dismantled. Reggie stripped and arrested. Wendy destroyed. Family bows. Marc bows. Full vindication.
Eps 351–400
Great War + Resolution
Border threat. Merida goes to war in Darian's place. Fae magic revealed. Great War. Twins born. All ends well.

Big pivot — ep 100
Underdog peaks early and fades fast. After ep 100 the dominant register shifts to Slow Burn + Political Intrigue. Ads that only play arc 1 mis-sell the bulk of the show.
Structural spine
Cinderella / Rise to Queen climbs ep 1 → ep 350 without dropping. It's the show's longest single promise. Hidden Power mirrors it — both are retention tropes, not acquisition.
Most underrated hook
Scheming Rival (Wendy) holds 35–45% for 250+ consecutive episodes — the most sustained conflict engine after romance. Consistently underfunded in both current ads.
Missing from both scripts
Slow Burn Intimacy and Weak-to-Strong Combat get zero coverage in either ad. These are the top two retention drivers for eps 100–400.
