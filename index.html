Lunar Curse
Overview · Story arcs · Dominant tropes

A werewolf romance spanning 400+ episodes. Merida Cooper, branded with the Lunar Curse at sixteen and rejected at the Lunar Ball, is taken as second-chance mate by Alpha Darian Oaksmith — a future king who wants nothing to do with the wolfless, cursed girl he has married. What begins as a political arrangement built on resentment slowly becomes the most transformative bond either will ever know.

Ep 1 to 10
Rejected & Claimed
Merida is rejected at the Lunar Ball, Darian takes her as second-chance mate
▸

The Lunar Ball was supposed to be Merida's night of destiny. Standing at the entrance of the Lunarstone Pack ballroom in black, veil covering her scarred cheek, her father hisses at her not to embarrass the family. She already knows what is coming. Marc Harris, future Beta, publicly and loudly rejects her in front of the entire pack: "I, Marc Harris, future Beta of the Lunarstone Pack, reject you, Merida Cooper, as my mate." The humiliation is total — her pheromones explode uncontrollably, her veil is torn from her face, the crowd sees her scar. Then Alpha Darian Oaksmith crosses the room, surveys the situation, and says four words that stop everything: "Then I will take her."

Within days Merida is married to Darian — Alpha of the Mystic Fury Pack and future King of the Werewolves — and installed at his estate. He resents the arrangement. His wolf Rhovan is less certain. She is assigned maids, given rooms, and treated with a cold professionalism that makes clear she is a political acquisition. Azul, the maid who cared for Merida as a child before her father dismissed her, reappears as Darian's wedding gift from her mother. It is the most ambiguous kindness Merida has ever received.

Ep 11 to 20
Duskmoor Begins
Life at the estate, Xena appears, Rhovan acknowledges the bond
▸

Merida settles into Duskmoor, Darian's estate, surrounded by a household staff that ranges from carefully neutral to quietly hostile. Wendy, Xena's nanny, is an early source of friction. Xena — Darian's young daughter — is the first person in the house to reach for Merida without agenda. Their bond develops without drama: Xena simply attaches herself, and Merida, who has spent her whole life being unwanted, does not quite know what to do with a child who wants her company.

Darian watches all of this from a careful distance. His wolf Rhovan is increasingly vocal about the mate bond's reality. Darian dismisses it. He has a mission that requires a strategic marriage, not a genuine one, and he has talked himself into believing these are the same thing. He is not yet right about that. Merida, for her part, asks nothing of him and makes herself useful through observation, which unsettles him more than demands would.

Ep 21 to 30
Tempest & the Pool
Merida almost drowns saving Xena; Darian dives in
▸

Xena, wanting her father's attention, releases Tempest — a dangerous horse — and the situation spirals toward disaster. Merida is thrown into the pool in the chaos and cannot swim. She thrashes, goes under, vision blackening. Darian, alerted by Rhovan's frantic signal, sprints from the house and dives in without hesitation. He pulls her out and performs mouth-to-mouth while Azul collapses on the tiles next to her, screaming her name. Merida comes back. Xena stands at the edge of the pool staring, wide-eyed.

The aftermath is complicated. Darian confronts Xena with a controlled fury that hides fear. "I just wanted your attention," she whispers, and the anger drains out of him. He sits on the bed beside her. Outside, Merida is being fussed over by maids and does not mention the incident again. She has learned that the way to survive is to treat close calls as ordinary. Darian notices this habit and files it away.

Ep 31 to 40
Record Straight
Darian's true intentions surface; Rhovan pushes harder on the bond
▸

Merida begins to piece together what Darian's mission actually is — not just political leverage, but something larger involving the five Alpha packs and the royal throne. He does not explain himself to her, but the gaps in his explanations are increasingly legible. She does not confront him. She accumulates information the way she has always accumulated it: quietly, systematically, without announcing what she is doing.

Rhovan escalates his pressure on Darian about the mate bond. Their internal arguments — conducted in Darian's head with a wolf who is equally proud and considerably more honest — become a running argument that Darian is consistently losing. He does not concede. He simply finds new reasons to defer, which Rhovan demolishes with methodical patience. Merida, who knows none of this is happening, continues not asking questions she can see he will not answer. He finds this infuriating.

Ep 41 to 50
Rhovan's Suggestion
Valmora speaks for the first time; Merida discovers she has a wolf
▸

Merida is soaking in the bath when a voice speaks in her head with the aggressive certainty of someone who has been waiting a long time to be heard. Valmora, her wolf, is present, has always been present, and has significant opinions about the choices Merida has been making. The first conversation is not warm. Valmora tells her she has been wasting her life and holding her own wolf back with her own hands. Merida, who expected sympathy, gets an assignment instead.

"Why didn't you show up sooner?" Merida demands. "I've always been here. I just needed a trigger to come out," Valmora replies. The trigger, it turns out, was Darian specifically. Not sleeping with any man — Darian. Merida does not know how to process this information and decides not to. The crescent moon mark, she notices, has not disappeared. The curse is not yet broken.

Ep 51 to 60
Finding Truths
Valmora's nature deepens; Darian trains Merida seriously
▸

Merida begins training under Danny — Darian's brother — who is simultaneously the person most invested in not seeing her fail and the most relentlessly honest about how far she has to go. Their sessions are physically demanding and they develop an easy rapport built on mutual competitiveness and her refusal to complain. Danny offers to get her anything she wants if she lands one clean hit on him. She takes the deal.

Valmora continues to talk to Merida with the impatience of someone who has been waiting years for her host to start making better decisions. She refuses to answer questions directly and insists Merida earn her answers by working. Merida, who has always prided herself on not asking for help, finds this arrangement deeply irritating and also, reluctantly, correct. Darian watches her training from a distance and says nothing.

Ep 61 to 70
Valmora & Rhovan Collide
The wolves argue; Darian locks Rhovan out of his own head
▸

In Darian's study, Valmora's voice erupts into his mind without invitation — calling Rhovan a "sneaky, lying bastard" with the fury of someone delivering a long-overdue verdict. Rhovan shuts her out. They both vanish from Darian's consciousness, apparently to conduct their argument in private. Darian is left alone in his study, locked out of his own wolf, which has never happened before. He sits by the fire and stares at the wall.

What Rhovan is hiding, what Valmora knows about him that produces that level of rage, and how Merida fits into the history between these two wolves — these threads are now visible as threads. Darian's conclusion: he must train Merida to control Valmora, because if she cannot, Valmora will devour her. And he must find out what Rhovan is lying about before it becomes a problem he cannot manage.

Ep 71 to 80
The Wildflower Crown
Xena crowns Merida "My Queen"; Darian hears it through the bond
▸

In the garden, Xena weaves a crown of wildflowers with the seriousness of a priestess performing a ritual. She places it on Merida's head and steps back. "There. Now you look like a real Queen." Merida, who has only ever heard that word applied to her by Valmora — and never by a person who meant it without an agenda — pulls Xena onto her lap and holds her. Cora, the senior maid, appears and tells her the crown suits her.

Darian, on the terrace with Danny, hears it through Rhovan's awareness of the mate bond — the shape of the moment without the words — and cuts Danny off mid-laugh. Danny reads the room immediately and stops talking. Gerry, Merida's brother, has been punished by Danny for harassment at the training grounds. Everything is accelerating.

Ep 81 to 100
The Cooper Siblings Arrive
Mabel and Gerry sent to Duskmoor; Merida's childhood trauma surfaces
▸

Margaret Cooper sends Mabel and Gerry to Duskmoor — officially as family, actually as surveillance at Gabriel's instruction. The effect on Merida is immediate and specific. She does not flinch visibly, but Darian notices the way she recalibrates herself when they are in the room. That evening, she finally talks to him about her childhood. Not all at once, and not easily, but the shape of it comes out: the pinching, the hiding of her things, the years of cruelty she mistook for ordinary sibling meanness, and then the complete erasure of warmth from her parents after the curse hit at sixteen.

"Before the Lunar Curse, they adored me," she tells him. "And then the curse happened. And just like that, they changed." Darian listens without speaking. His jaw is locked. His hands are still. He promises her something and means it more than she knows yet.

Ep 101 to 150
Darian's Mother
Merida meets Darian's mother; the prophecy is delivered
▸

Merida meets Darian's mother for the first time. She grasps Merida's hand with the warmth of someone welcoming home a person she has been expecting. Then, with a clarity that cuts through her usual confusion, she looks at Merida and says: "You will bring about our end." Before anyone can respond, she laughs — bright, hearty, entirely at odds with what she just said — and adds: "But it doesn't matter. As long as my son is safe."

Darian and Danny move immediately to redirect her. Merida sits with the weight of those words. Valmora, unusually, says only: Your feelings are valid. Which is the most unsettling response possible, because Valmora is not normally gentle. Something in the prophecy is true, and both wolves know it.

Ep 151 to 200
Training & Trust
Merida lands her first clean hit; the bond deepens outside the ring
▸

The training arc comes to its first milestone. With Danny circling her and Darian watching from the edge of the courtyard, Merida lands a clean block that Danny himself acknowledges. The session ends with him producing ice cream as promised. Darian, who made no promises, finds himself standing there after everyone else has left, processing something he does not yet have words for.

The mate bond is active enough now that both of them feel the edge of the other's emotions during heightened moments. Darian tries to interpret this as strategic information. Rhovan treats it as confirmation of something obvious that his alpha has been refusing to see for a hundred episodes. Their internal arguments have graduated from Rhovan presenting evidence to Rhovan presenting evidence that Darian has already conceded privately and won't concede out loud.

Ep 201 to 250
The Seal Revealed
Merida learns the Lunar Curse was a Fae seal placed to protect her identity
▸

Merida's grandmother leads her to a moonlit clearing where Fae women are gathered. What follows rewrites everything Merida understood about herself. The Lunar Curse was never a curse. It was a seal, placed by the Fae to hide her true nature from people who would kill her for it. She is the reincarnation of Serena, the Wolf Queen, and Valmora is Serena's wolf. The crescent moon mark disappears from her shoulder. She can shift.

Merida tells Darian everything. He listens without interrupting. When she finishes, he asks only one question: "How did you connect with Valmora before the seal broke?" She answers: "Because we're mated." He sits with that. He does not answer immediately. He is a man who does not say things until he has thought them through completely. What he is thinking about has nothing to do with doubt.

Ep 251 to 300
The Capital & the Queen
Merida navigates Queen Loraina; the King is revealed to be poisoned
▸

At court, Merida walks with Queen Loraina through the palace halls. The Queen has heard all the rumors: wolfless, cursed, weak, a disgrace. She studies Merida and revises her assessment without embarrassment. "You are certainly not weak. That much is clear." The conversation turns to Merida's public rejection at the Lunar Ball. Merida does not flinch. The Queen tells her that Darian's path to the throne is certain, and walks her through halls she will one day stand in as Queen. Merida says she is walking her path on her own terms. The Queen understands exactly what that means.

In private, Loraina discloses that the King has been poisoned — a slow-acting agent, administered over a long period. The identity of the poisoner is unknown. The truth must remain secret or it will incite chaos. Merida's hands curl into fists. She asks who would dare harm the King. Loraina's voice drops. She does not have an answer yet. Someone inside the court is responsible.

Ep 301 to 350
The Coronation
Darian becomes King; Merida faces Marc at the coronation as Queen
▸

Darian is crowned King of Stormveil. Merida stands beside him. The ceremony brings together all five Alpha packs including Marc Harris, who bows before her and does not rise until she allows it. She lets him stay bowed longer than necessary, then asks, "And you are?" — as if she cannot quite place the face. He swallows and gives his name. She sizes him up for what feels like a long time and tells him plainly that leadership is about character. He leaves considerably diminished.

Darian, managing the political theatre of the coronation, deals with Levi — a pack Elder who attempts to petition him during the feast. Darian's refusal is absolute and calm: "It is not a day for petitions." Merida watches and whispers that he might grow to resent Darian for that. Darian says: "My decision stands." They have become something that functions. Not just as mates but as partners who understand each other's read of a room.

Ep 351 to 400+
Vampires at the Gate
Xena's shapeshifting confirmed; vampire attack closes on the palace
▸

Xena — who has been moving through the story as a child with slightly too much awareness and a suspicious relationship with timing — is in the air above the capital city in bird form when she sees the vampire groups closing on the palace. She shifts back mid-flight, slips through a high window as Azul, and finds Darian in the Grand Council Chamber. When the guard finally admits her, she drops the Azul mask: "There's a problem." Darian's face goes to stone. "Where?" She meets his eyes. "Here. The Palace."

Merida, at the border inspecting the Rampart, feels the shift in the wind. She has known something was coming — Valmora has been tracking the shape of it for weeks. The final arc is about what happens when the oldest power in the world surfaces in a political structure that was built to suppress exactly that.
